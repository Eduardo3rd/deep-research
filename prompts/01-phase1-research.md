## Phase 1: Company Research Dossier

Phase 1 builds a comprehensive, lens-agnostic research dossier on the target company. This research is reusable — it serves as the foundation for investment due diligence, sales account planning, or any other downstream analysis.

### Output Structure

Create the following files in the project directory:

| File | Topic | Priority |
|------|-------|----------|
| `01-company-overview.md` | Founding, mission, history, financials, facilities | P0 |
| `02-leadership-org.md` | Founders, C-suite, board, org chart, key hires | P0 |
| `03-products-technology.md` | Product suite, tech stack, manufacturing, R&D | P0 |
| `04-market-landscape.md` | TAM/SAM/SOM, market dynamics, regulations, trends | P0 |
| `05-competitive-landscape.md` | Competitors, positioning, feature matrix, pricing | P0 |
| `06-business-model.md` | Revenue model, pricing, unit economics, margins | P0 |
| `07-financial-analysis.md` | Revenue, margins, burn, funding history, capital efficiency | P1 |
| `08-customers-segments.md` | Customer base, segments, case studies, churn signals | P1 |
| `09-go-to-market.md` | Sales motion, marketing, partnerships, channels | P1 |
| `10-news-sentiment.md` | Recent events, press coverage, analyst views, triggers | P1 |
| `11-talent-culture.md` | Employee sentiment, hiring, compensation, culture | P2 |
| `12-risks-opportunities.md` | Risk framework + growth vectors | P2 |

---

### Module 1: Company Overview

**Required Elements:**
- Company name, founding date, location, original name (if different)
- Founder backgrounds and founding motivation
- Mission and vision statements (exact quotes)
- Key milestones timeline (product launches, funding, customer wins, pivots)
- Current headquarters and all office/facility locations
- Employee count trajectory over time
- Revenue (actual or estimated) and recent financial performance
- Key business segments
- Geographic footprint and markets served
- Major customers (if B2B) and key suppliers

**Advanced Research:**
- Stealth period activities (if applicable)
- Early customers before public launch
- Evolution of positioning/messaging over time
- Original cap table structure (if discoverable)
- Manufacturing capabilities and production processes
- Regulatory status (FDA approvals, ISO certs, etc.)

### Module 2: Leadership & Organization

**Required Elements:**
- Full biography of each founder (education, prior companies, exits)
- C-suite profiles (tenure, background, compensation if public)
- Board composition and governance
- Key executive hires in past 24 months
- Notable departures and reasons
- Organizational structure (divisions, reporting lines)
- VP/Director-level leaders by function (Engineering, Quality, Manufacturing, Sales, etc.)

**Advanced Research:**
- Founder track record (previous company outcomes with specifics)
- "Spike" analysis — what makes this team uniquely suited?
- Network mapping (shared investors, board members, prior colleagues)
- Public statements revealing strategic thinking
- Speaking engagements and thought leadership
- Any controversies or legal issues involving leadership
- Ownership concentration (founders, VCs, employees)

### Module 3: Products & Technology

**Required Elements:**
- Complete product/service catalog with descriptions
- Pricing tiers and packaging
- Technical architecture overview
- Key differentiating capabilities
- Product roadmap signals
- API/integration ecosystem
- Patent portfolio analysis
- Manufacturing/production processes (if applicable)
- Quality and inspection methods used
- Equipment and tooling (specific systems, vendors)

**Advanced Research:**
- Technology stack (infrastructure, languages, frameworks)
- Build vs. buy decisions
- Technical debt indicators
- Platform vs. point solution positioning
- Developer experience and documentation quality
- Open source contributions and research publications
- Automation level and Industry 4.0 adoption
- NDT/testing capabilities and gaps

### Module 4: Market Landscape

**Required Elements:**
- TAM/SAM/SOM with methodology
- Market growth rate and drivers
- Industry value chain mapping
- Buyer personas and decision process
- Regulatory environment (FDA, ISO, industry-specific)
- Geographic market breakdown
- Cyclicality and seasonality

**Advanced Research:**
- Market timing analysis — why now?
- Adjacent market opportunities
- Technology disruption vectors
- Consolidation trends
- Private equity / strategic interest in space
- IPO/M&A activity in sector
- New product introduction (NPI) trends

### Module 5: Competitive Landscape

**Required Elements:**
- Competitive landscape map (direct, indirect, future)
- Feature/capability comparison matrix
- Pricing comparison
- Go-to-market comparison
- Funding/resource comparison
- Win/loss patterns (if discoverable)
- Installed equipment and vendor relationships

**Advanced Research:**
- Competitor response to company's moves
- Switching cost analysis
- Customer overlap analysis
- Talent flow between competitors
- Patent/IP disputes
- Competitive intelligence from job postings
- Competitor weaknesses and vulnerabilities

### Module 6: Business Model

**Required Elements:**
- Revenue model (subscription, usage, services, hardware, hybrid)
- Pricing architecture and packaging
- Contract structure (length, terms, renewal)
- Unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback, if discoverable)
- Revenue composition (by product, segment, geography)
- Gross margin analysis
- Path to profitability

**Advanced Research:**
- Pricing power indicators
- Land-and-expand dynamics
- Net revenue retention estimation
- Professional services dependency
- Revenue quality (recurring vs. one-time)
- Customer concentration risks

### Module 7: Financial Analysis

**Required Elements:**
- Revenue history and growth trajectory
- Funding history (all rounds, amounts, investors, valuations)
- Burn rate and runway estimates
- Profitability metrics (gross margin, operating margin)
- Balance sheet health
- Capital efficiency ratios

**Advanced Research:**
- Cohort economics (if discoverable)
- Unit economics by segment
- Working capital dynamics
- Debt structure and covenants
- Equity dilution analysis
- Valuation benchmarking against peers

### Module 8: Customers & Segments

**Required Elements:**
- Named customer list (comprehensive)
- Customer segmentation (industry, size, geography)
- Logo acquisition timeline
- Case studies with ROI metrics
- Customer testimonials and quotes
- Churn indicators

**Advanced Research:**
- Customer concentration analysis
- Largest customer identification
- Customer satisfaction signals (NPS, reviews)
- Customer expansion patterns
- Win/loss reasons
- Reference-worthy customer relationships

### Module 9: Go-to-Market

**Required Elements:**
- Sales model (self-serve, inside sales, field sales, channel)
- Sales team structure and size
- Marketing strategy and channels
- Partnership ecosystem
- Sales cycle length
- Geographic expansion strategy

**Advanced Research:**
- Sales productivity metrics (quota attainment, ramp time)
- Marketing efficiency (CAC by channel)
- Partner contribution to revenue
- Enterprise vs. SMB motion
- International GTM challenges
- Sales leader tenure and track record

### Module 10: News & Sentiment

**Required Elements:**
- Media coverage timeline (last 24 months)
- Key narrative themes
- Analyst coverage and ratings
- Social media presence and thought leadership
- Awards and recognition
- Recent events creating urgency or risk

**Advanced Research:**
- Sentiment analysis over time
- Coverage quality vs. quantity
- Narrative evolution
- Competitor share of voice
- Crisis communication history
- Buying/investment trigger events

### Module 11: Talent & Culture

**Required Elements:**
- Glassdoor/Blind ratings and trends
- Employee count and growth trajectory
- Key roles being hired (reveals strategic priorities)
- Compensation benchmarking
- Diversity metrics (if disclosed)
- Culture indicators

**Advanced Research:**
- RepVue sales team sentiment
- Levels.fyi compensation data
- LinkedIn talent flow analysis
- Hiring velocity by department
- Employee tenure patterns
- Alumni outcomes

### Module 12: Risks & Opportunities

**Required Elements:**

*Risks:*
- Technology risks
- Market risks
- Execution risks
- Financial risks
- Competitive risks
- Regulatory risks
- Key person risks
- Customer concentration risks

*Opportunities:*
- Geographic expansion
- Vertical expansion
- Product expansion
- M&A opportunities
- Partnership opportunities
- Platform evolution potential

**Advanced Research:**
- Risk severity matrix (likelihood x impact)
- Historical risk materialization in sector
- Mitigation strategies assessment
- Prioritization framework for opportunities
- Resource requirements for each growth path
- Timing considerations and competitive response likelihood
